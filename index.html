AI Powered Industrial Robotics

GRC Workshop 2026

Abstract

This workshop provides a platform for doctoral researchers to present their work to a diverse audience of professors, postdocs, fellow doctoral students, and industry representatives. Presentations are designed to foster technical feedback, critical discussion, and mentorship on research design, methodology, and application relevance. By bringing together senior and junior researchers across RIG partner institutions, the workshop promotes a shared understanding of scientific challenges, industrial constraints, and evaluation standards. This alignment contributes to a coherent national research roadmap and strengthens RIG’s role as a coordinating hub for industrial robotics research in Germany.

The workshop emphasizes practical, application-driven outcomes. Participants are encouraged to highlight industrial relevance, including challenges related to robustness, scalability, and real-world deployment. Interaction with industry representatives ensures that research directions are grounded in practical requirements, accelerates the translation of scientific advances into deployable solutions, and enhances the visibility of emerging research within the RIG ecosystem.

The technical focus of the workshop targets industrial robotics with direct factory relevance. Contributions are expected to address challenges in applying AI in real industrial settings, and to include practical evaluations of methods, systems, or prototypes.

Key topics of interest include:

  • Transfer of AI technologies into industrial robotic systems
  • Software engineering approaches and scaling strategies for AI-based robotics
  • Skill-based robotics and modular control concepts
  • Data ecosystems for industrial automation
